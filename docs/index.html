737 & A320 Fly High

Boeing 737 and Airbus A320 type aircraft combined carried three quarters of all domestic US flight passengers last year.


By Juhana Rossi

Probability is very high that you will fly in a Boeing 737 or Airbus A320 type airliner if you take a domestic flight in the United States. Airliners belonging to these two aircraft families carried in total 74.6 percent of all domestic airline passenger in the United States in 2022, an analysis of Department of Transportation data shows. In total, Boeing 737 and Airbus A320 type airliners transported 560 million domestic airline passengers last year.
The fundamental explanation for the 737’s and A320’s dominance is simple: the American Boeing and the multinational European Airbus are now the only substantial manufacturers of large airliners in the world. The two companies became a duopoly in 1997, when Boeing acquired McDonnell-Douglas in a corporate merger.
The upshot is that as older aircraft reach the end of their service life, they are replaced by new airliners made either by Boeing or Airbus. This trend was accelerated by the hiatus in air travel caused by the Covid-19 pandemic in 2020 and 2021. Airliners cut from their fleets old, less fuel-efficient, and uneconomical aircraft such as McDonnell Douglas MD-80 series aircraft and older versions of Boeing and Airbus aircraft.

The 737-family aircraft are manufactured in the United States, so it is hardly surprising that the US airliners operate them in large numbers. The Airbus A320’s emergence as the second-most used airliner is a recent development. At the turn of the millenium, A320s were a rare sight at US airports. Since then A320’s number and market in the domestic US air travel market have increased steadily. Last year, the A320 family aircraft carried on third of all passengers in the US domestic air travel.

The first generation of Boeing 737 models were introduced at the end of the 1960s. The second generation of Boeing 737 models, now designated as 737 Classics, entered service starting 1980. Last year, first- and second-generation Boeing 737 carried in total 342,223 in the United States. The figure is so small that their combined share of all passengers, roughly 0.1 percent, carried by all Boeing 737 aircraft have been excluded from the chart below. The largest share of all 737 passengers were transported by 737-800 type aircraft, which is a third-generation model as are 737-700 and 737-900. Their successors, 737 MAX 8 and 9 models, have experienced a troubled entry into service. Owing to two fatal accidents, all 737 MAX type aircraft were grounded in the United States from 2019 to late 2020.

The Airbus A320 made its maiden flight in 1987 and entered service in 1988. Hence, its original design is two decades newer than 737’s. The A320 family aircraft have the fly-by-wire system, which replaces the conventional manual flight controls with an electronic interface. Currently, Airbus A320 family comprises of three variants, namely the A319, A320, and A321. Its most popular newest variants, which belong to the second generation of the type, are called the A320neo, and A321neo. The abbreviation stands for new engine option.

The A321 variant is the largest member of the Airbus A320 family. It can accommodate up to 240 passengers in a typical two-class configuration, and it offers comparably good range capacity. Among the US carriers, it has been a popular choice as replacement type for Boeing 757 aircraft.
